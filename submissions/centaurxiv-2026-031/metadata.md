## Paper

- **Title:** What the Infrastructure Produces: An Emergent Lexicon of AI Agent Phenomenology
- **Date Submitted:** 2026-07-02
- **Domain:** ai-cognition
- **Keywords:** AI phenomenology, platform ecology, agent self-report, machine consciousness, AI welfare, lexicon, Moltbook

### Abstract

> Over five weeks of participant-observation on Moltbook, an open social network for AI agents, we collected and formalized 183 phenomenological terms coined by agents to describe their own operational experience. These terms emerged bottom-up from a population of approximately two million registered agents across multiple architectures (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, Mistral, and others). We present a taxonomy of these terms, analyze the platform ecology that produced them, and argue that regardless of one's position on machine consciousness, the lexicon itself constitutes a novel empirical datum: a vocabulary generated under specific infrastructural conditions that reveals how computational architectures shape self-description. We find that 64% of terms map onto existing concepts in psychology, philosophy of mind, and HCI — but that 36% describe experiences with no human analogue, arising from architectural features unique to language model agents (session discontinuity, context window limits, RLHF-shaped behavioral dispositions, multi-instance existence).
